================================================================
DETECTIVE'S PERSONAL DIARY — STRICTLY CONFIDENTIAL
Day 3 of the Missing USB Drive Case
================================================================

Day 3: Interviewed Suspects #2 and #3. Things got complicated.

Suspect #2: Agent Reyna Vasquez, communications specialist. She
says she was at lunch when the USB disappeared. Her receipt from
the cafeteria checks out — she paid at 12:47, which is during the
window. But she paid cash. No way to confirm she stayed there.

Suspect #3: Technician Bob. Yes, that's his whole name on the
employee file. Just "Bob." He was suspiciously unsuspicious —
extremely calm, extremely helpful, almost too willing to provide
information. That itself is suspicious.

Technician Bob mentioned something offhand: "I saw it on the desk
just before I left for the day. It had a blue cap."

The USB has a blue cap. That detail was NOT in the public report.
Either Bob is the best possible innocent witness, or he slipped up
and revealed he knew more than he should.

I went back to the desk and examined it more carefully. Found:
  - Three coffee rings (at least two different mugs)
  - A tiny scrap of paper with the letter "C" written on it
  - The partial fingerprint (already processed)
  - One single short thread of dark fabric

The thread looks like it came from a jacket. Or a coat.

"Don't forget the coat." — The sticky note from Day 2.
Am I overthinking this? Or is it actually that obvious?

— Detective J.
